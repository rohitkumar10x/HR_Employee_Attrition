# HR Employee Attrition Analysis (Power BI Project)

This project focuses on understanding the key reasons behind **employee attrition** using HR data.  
The Power BI dashboard highlights patterns related to **satisfaction level, salary, workload, tenure, promotions, and department performance**.

## 🔍 Dashboard Insights (Short & Important)

### 1. Salary Impact  
Employees in the **low salary group** show the highest attrition.

### 2. Promotion Impact  
Employees without promotion in the last 5 years leave more often.

### 3. Department Trends  
Sales, Technical, and Support departments face the **highest attrition**.

### 4. Tenure Trend  
Employees with **4–5 years** of experience leave the most.

### 5. Satisfaction Comparison  
Stayed employees have **higher satisfaction scores** than left employees.

### 6. Work Hours Pattern  
Employees who left have **imbalanced work hours**, showing disengagement before exit.

- work_accident

- promotion_last_5years  
- Department  
- salary  

**A derived Status column was used:**  
- 1 → Left  
- 0 → Stayed  

---

## 📌 Conclusion

The dashboard clearly reveals that **low salary, no promotion, high-pressure departments, low satisfaction, and mid-tenure stage** are the major reasons for employee attrition.  
These insights can help HR teams reduce turnover and improve workforce stability.
