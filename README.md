# HR Employee Attrition Analysis

> **Data-Driven Insights for Workforce Stability & Retention Strategy.**

This project focuses on analyzing HR employee attrition data to identify key factors contributing to employees leaving a company. By leveraging data-driven insights, the goal is to predict potential attrition and implement proactive strategies to improve workforce stability and retention.

---

## 📋 Project Overview

### The Problem

Employee attrition, defined as the rate at which employees leave a company, poses significant challenges for organizations. High attrition rates lead to:

*   **Increased Costs:** Substantial expenses associated with hiring and training new personnel.
*   **Reduced Morale:** A negative impact on team morale and overall productivity.

### The Solution & Objective

This project proposes a data-driven solution to combat attrition. By analyzing relevant employee data, it aims to:

*   **Predict Attrition:** Identify employees who are at a higher risk of leaving the company.
*   **Proactive Intervention:** Enable HR to address underlying issues before employees decide to quit.
*   **Clear Insights:** Build a clean Power BI dashboard to visualize these insights, facilitating quick and informed HR actions.

## 📈 Key Findings & Insights

### 1. Salary & Promotion Impact

Compensation and career growth opportunities play a crucial role in employee retention:

*   **Salary Impact:** Employees in lower salary brackets exhibit the highest attrition rates. As salary increases, the likelihood of an employee leaving significantly decreases.
*   **Promotion Impact:** A lack of promotion within the last five years is strongly correlated with higher attrition. The **5-year mark** is identified as a critical window where the absence of career advancement often leads to employee exits.
*   **Insight:** Employees are more likely to leave when they feel undervalued in terms of compensation or perceive limited future growth opportunities within the organization.

### 2. Department & Tenure Trends

Attrition patterns vary across departments and employee tenure:

*   **High-Risk Departments:** Sales, Technical, and Support departments experience the highest attrition counts, suggesting higher pressure or specific challenges within these roles.
*   **Stable Areas:** Management and Research & Development (R&D) departments show lower attrition rates, indicating better stability.
*   **Tenure Risk Zone:** Employees with **4-5 years of experience** are most prone to leaving, often seeking new challenges or better growth prospects elsewhere. This period represents a critical stage where mid-career employees are at the highest risk of departure.
*   **Insight:** High-pressure roles and mid-career employees require additional support and engagement strategies to ensure retention.

### 3. Satisfaction & Workload

Employee satisfaction and workload balance are strong indicators of attrition risk:

*   **Satisfaction Level:** Employees who remain with the company consistently report higher satisfaction scores (average 61.3%). Conversely, those who leave show a significant decline in happiness prior to their departure.
*   **Workload Pattern:** Employees who left often exhibited imbalanced work hours, characterized by either extreme overwork or very low engagement (average 201 hours per month).
*   **Insight:** High-pressure departments tend to have more irregular work patterns, contributing to faster burnout. Low satisfaction serves as an early warning sign of disengagement or "quiet quitting." Maintaining a balance between employee happiness and workload is crucial for preventing attrition.

---

## ✅ Final Conclusion & Recommendations

### Problem Summary

The core drivers of the 23.8% attrition rate are identified as low pay, limited career advancement opportunities, and high-pressure work environments. The 4-5 year tenure mark represents a critical period where employee departures significantly increase.

### Recommendations

To address these issues and foster a more stable workforce, the following recommendations are proposed:

*   **Compensation & Growth:** Implement salary adjustments for low-paid roles and establish clear, defined 3-year promotion pathways to provide employees with visible career progression.
*   **Targeted Retention:** Prioritize "stay interviews" and wellness programs specifically for employees within the 4-5 year risk zone to proactively address their concerns and enhance engagement.

**Result:** By implementing these recommendations, the organization can expect a more stable, happier, and productive workforce, ultimately leading to reduced turnover costs and improved overall organizational health.
